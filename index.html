About Me

I’m a Digital Technology & Culture major who focuses on animation, illustration, and motion design. I work mostly in 2D because that’s where I feel the strongest connection to storytelling, color, and character expression, but I also use 3D tools when a project needs them. When I’m not designing digitally, you’ll usually find me sketching or painting. Traditional art is a big part of how I develop my style and creative voice.

I’ve built small projects with HTML, CSS, and beginner JavaScript, and I’ve created logos and other graphic pieces that show my range as a designer. My process always centers on mood, color, and movement, finding ways to make every piece feel alive and full of personality.

T1 TimeStream

T1 TimeStream is an augmented reality project created for the Port of Vancouver. It brings historical stories from Vancouver’s Terminal 1 waterfront to life through interactive AR vignettes.

I worked as a 2D Illustrator and Motion Designer, creating visual assets for the AR scenes:
• Designed the train artwork (sketches, shading, colors, motion style)
• Created 2D scene elements used in the AR animations
• Followed the team’s mood board and visual guidelines
• Collaborated with teammates to refine assets for the final experience
